Liverpool premiere of A Hard Day's Night

July 10, 1964

On this day The Beatles released their first movie A Hard Day's Night , a movie following through with their new album A Hard Day's Night at the start of their career this was a commercial success, leading them to make more films connected to their albums in the future (i.e Help!, Magical Mystery Tour, and Yellow Submarine). Source - The Paul McCartney Project

Shea Stadium Concert

August 15th, 1965

On this date throughout The Beatles career they performed at Shea Stadium to a total of 55,000 people in the audience, this performance had been the highlight of the bands tour. From the performance each member had stated something: "It's like this: you make a noise and they make a noise, and it's the noise together that counts. It's the bible, really, with Cecil B. De Mille and 60,000 extras." - Paul , "It was marvellous. It was the biggest crowd we ever played to, anywhere in the world. It was the biggest live show anybody's ever done, they told us. And it was fantastic, the most exciting we've done. They could almost hear us as well, even though they were making a lot of noise, because the amplification was tremendous." - John, "Shea Stadium was an enormous place. In those days, people were still playing the Astoria Cinema at Finsbury Park. This was the first time that one of those stadiums was used for a rock concert. Vox made special big 100-watt amplifiers for that tour. We went up from the 30-watt amp to the 100-watt amp and it obviously wasn't enough; we just had the house PA." - George, "I never felt people came to hear our show - I felt they came to see us. From the count-in on the first number, the volume of screams drowned everything else out." - Ringo. Sources : Source 1 - The Beatles ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ Source 2 - Beatles at Shea Stadium video

Please Please Me Album recorded in a single day

February 11, 1963

This album had been recorded in a 10 hour session obtaining 10 out of the 14 tracks on the album, the final tracks were finalized in the days following; so technically it wasn't created in a day but the majority was! The band was in the middle of a tour and George Martin wanted them to record so they could push out the album quickly. Source - Udiscovermusic

First Abbey Road Session

June 6th, 1962

Here they have their first session at the infamous Abbey Road! Here they were able to have a successful recording and get put on the label thanks to Brian Epstien and George Martin. Martin was incredibly impressed with the song Love Me Do . Source - The Beatle's Bible

The Beatles audition at Decca

January 1st, 1962

This audition had been one of their biggest mistakes throughout music history, being a famous rejection. Decca claimed that guitar groups were on their way out and wouldn't sign them. Source - Wikepedia

First performance at The Cavern Club

August 7th, 1957

Under The Quarrymen this was the first performance done at The Cavern Club, these performances were integral for their commercial success in the future. Source - The Beatle’s Bible

George Harrison meets The Quarrymen!

February 6th , 1958

George Harrison met The Quarrymen on this date though he wouldn't join just yet this was one of the first interactions he had had with John and Paul. Source - The Beatle’s Bible

The Quarrymen have formed!

Before The Beatles there was The Quarrymen ! During this time they would do small local gigs, both John and Paul. They didn’t know how big of a band they’d soon become! There's also an AWESOME movie about the foundation of The Quarrymen it's called Nowhere Boy. Source - The Beatles Wiki
