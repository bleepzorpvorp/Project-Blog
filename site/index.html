Shea Stadium Concert

August 15th, 1965

On this date throughout The Beatles career they performed at Shea Stadium to a total of 55,000 people in the audience, this performance had been the highlight of the bands tour. From the performance each member had stated something: "It's like this: you make a noise and they make a noise, and it's the noise together that counts. It's the bible, really, with Cecil B. De Mille and 60,000 extras." - Paul , "It was marvellous. It was the biggest crowd we ever played to, anywhere in the world. It was the biggest live show anybody's ever done, they told us. And it was fantastic, the most exciting we've done. They could almost hear us as well, even though they were making a lot of noise, because the amplification was tremendous." - John, "Shea Stadium was an enormous place. In those days, people were still playing the Astoria Cinema at Finsbury Park. This was the first time that one of those stadiums was used for a rock concert. Vox made special big 100-watt amplifiers for that tour. We went up from the 30-watt amp to the 100-watt amp and it obviously wasn't enough; we just had the house PA." - George, "I never felt people came to hear our show - I felt they came to see us. From the count-in on the first number, the volume of screams drowned everything else out." - Ringo. Sources : Source 1 - The Beatles ‎ ‎ ‎ ‎ Source 2 - Beatles at Shea Stadium video
